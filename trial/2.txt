### [3]. Nvidia Launches Revenue-Sharing and Credit-Support Program for AI Startups

**Source:** Bloomberg / MLQ News / WinBuzzer
**Event Date:** 2026-07-03 | **Article Date:** 2026-07-03
**Status:** Official Announcement
**Primary URL:** https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-02/nvidia-launches-revenue-share-program-for-ai-startups

#### [Overview]
Nvidia has introduced a groundbreaking revenue-sharing and credit-support model for AI startups and regional cloud providers, shifting its monetization strategy beyond one-time hardware sales. Under the program, co-authored by CFO Colette Kress, Nvidia will provide token credits and access to its high-demand graphics processing units (GPUs) in exchange for a percentage of future cloud revenue generated by those chips. The first two partners under the program are Australia-based Sharon AI and Singapore-based Firmus Technologies, who plan to deploy a combined 210,000 Nvidia Grace Blackwell GB300 GPUs. Sharon AI has signed a six-year agreement covering 72 MW of new data center capacity built to Nvidia's DSX AI factory design, scaling to 40,000 GPUs. Firmus Technologies is developing a 360 MW DSX AI Factory campus in Batam, Indonesia, designed to house up to 170,000 GPUs. This financial innovation addresses a critical financing gap, allowing capital-constrained startups to access frontier compute without massive upfront capital expenditures.

#### [Strategic Impact]

- **Technology & Ecosystem Perspective:** Redefines the relationship between hardware vendors and software developers, turning Nvidia into an active financial intermediary and platform provider with recurring, usage-linked revenue.
- **Business & Market Dynamics:** Bypasses traditional hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) by building a direct, equity-like relationship with independent AI developers and regional neoclouds.
- **Infrastructure & Hardware Implications:** Accelerates the deployment of massive, localized data centers in emerging markets (such as Batam, Indonesia) built strictly on Nvidia's DSX AI factory reference designs.
- **Investment Strategy:** Highly bullish for independent neoclouds and hardware suppliers like Everpure, which provides high-performance flash storage arrays optimized for massive GPU clusters.

#### [Technical Deep Dive]

- **Core Technical Specifications (SW/HW):** [Sharon AI Capacity]: [40,000 Grace Blackwell GB300 GPUs / 72 MW] | [Firmus Technologies Capacity]: [170,000 Grace Blackwell GB300 GPUs / 360 MW] | [NVIDIA Reference Design]: [DSX AI Factory] | **Source:** Bloomberg / MLQ News
- **Comparative Analysis:** Unlike traditional hardware sales that generate one-time capital expenditure revenue, this model allows Nvidia to "double-dip" by collecting upfront product revenue from cloud partners and a recurring percentage of downstream cloud income.
- **Release / Deployment Stage:** [Program officially launched; first deployments with Sharon AI and Firmus Technologies underway]

#### [Critical View]
While this model lowers the barrier to entry for startups, it represents an aggressive expansion of Nvidia's monopoly power. By taking a cut of future revenue, Nvidia effectively reaches into its customers' income statements, creating a high-margin lock-in that could stifle independent software innovation and inflate the cost of AI compute.

#### [Overview]
Global consulting firm Sia has joined a 6 million EUR (approximately 7 million USD) funding round in Paris-based startup Lemrock, positioning itself at the center of what both companies see as the next structural shift in digital commerce: the rise of AI agents as primary shopping interfaces [3]. Founded in 2025, Lemrock builds a specialized middleware layer that structures product data so large language models (LLMs) can retrieve, interpret, and display it within conversational interfaces like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity [3]. As consumers increasingly migrate away from traditional search engines and brand-owned websites to ask AI agents for product recommendations and comparisons, retailers are facing up to 30% declines in direct web traffic [3]. Lemrock's platform addresses this "discovery gap" by providing the infrastructure required for brands to feed structured product data directly into agentic reasoning loops and execute autonomous transactions [3]. The investment round, led by Galion.exe, underscores a growing investor conviction that conversational AI is rapidly evolving from a simple information discovery layer into an active, transactional commerce environment [3].

#### [Strategic Impact]

- **Technology & Ecosystem Perspective:** Accelerates the transition toward "Agentic Commerce," where the primary interface for digital transactions shifts from human-centric web browsers to autonomous, machine-to-machine API integrations [3].
- **Business & Market Dynamics:** Traditional search engine optimization (SEO) and digital advertising models are threatened with obsolescence as AI agents act as gatekeepers to consumer purchasing decisions, forcing brands to adopt Lemrock's middleware to remain visible [3].
- **Infrastructure & Hardware Implications:** Processing real-time product inventories, pricing, and transactional data across millions of autonomous agent queries will drive demand for highly scalable, low-latency API gateways and secure transaction layers [3].
- **Investment Strategy:** Highly bullish for middleware startups building the transactional and data-routing infrastructure for AI agents. Investors should pivot capital away from traditional ad-tech and SEO agencies toward agentic enablement platforms [3].

**Event Date:** 2026-07-03 | **Article Date:** 2026-07-03
**Status:** Official Announcement
**Primary URL:** https://www.forbes.com/sites/siladityaray/2026/07/03/aisa-raises-65-million-seed-funding-led-by-alibaba-and-tribe-capital/

#### [Overview]
San Francisco-based payment startup AIsa has raised 6.5 million USD in seed funding, led by Alibaba and Tribe Capital, to build an "Amazon for agents". The company addresses a significant bottleneck in the emerging agentic economy: the inability of AI agents to autonomously transact on human-designed digital platforms. AIsa's solution is a unified transaction layer and programmable interface that allows AI agents to discover, access, and pay for digital resources like data and APIs at scale. The platform features a usage-based billing system that settles in fiat or stablecoins, enabling autonomous "AI employees" to execute financial transactions without human intervention. The funding round highlights a growing venture capital focus on the transactional infrastructure required to support autonomous agentic workflows as they transition from simple task automation to complex, commercial operations.

#### [Strategic Impact]

- **Technology & Ecosystem Perspective:** Establishes the foundational payment infrastructure for the "Agentic Economy," enabling machine-to-machine microtransactions and autonomous resource procurement.
- **Business & Market Dynamics:** Creates a new marketplace model where digital resources are dynamically priced, discovered, and purchased by autonomous software agents, bypassing traditional human-centric procurement pipelines.
- **Infrastructure & Hardware Implications:** Running continuous, high-frequency microtransactions across millions of autonomous agents will drive demand for highly secure, low-latency transaction processing nodes and decentralized ledger integrations.
- **Investment Strategy:** Highly bullish for agentic payment rails, API marketplaces, and usage-based billing platforms. Investors should prioritize startups building the transactional plumbing for autonomous software.

#### [Technical Deep Dive]

- **Core Technical Specifications (SW/HW):** [Seed Funding]: [6.5 million USD] | [Lead Investors]: [Alibaba, Tribe Capital] | [Settlement Assets]: [Fiat and stablecoins] | **Source:** Forbes
- **Comparative Analysis:** Unlike traditional payment gateways (such as Stripe or PayPal) designed for human authentication and credit card processing, AIsa provides a programmatic, machine-to-machine microtransaction layer optimized for LLM-driven agents.
- **Release / Deployment Stage:** [Seed round closed; transaction layer platform in early developer beta]

#### [Critical View]
Giving autonomous AI agents the ability to execute financial transactions introduces severe security and liability risks.
