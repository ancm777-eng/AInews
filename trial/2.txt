**Primary URL:** https://www.lasvegassun.com/news/2026/jul/08/meta-plans-billions-for-first-ai-data-center-in-canada-largest-outside-the-us/

#### [Overview]
Meta announced on Wednesday, July 8, 2026, that it will invest more than 9.1 billion USD to build its first artificial intelligence data center in Canada, which will also be its largest facility outside the United States. Located in Sturgeon County, Alberta, the massive facility addresses the critical physical bottleneck of power grid capacity. Because Alberta's local electricity grid cannot support multiple large AI data centers, the province's regulatory framework prioritizes projects that secure their own power generation. To meet this requirement, Meta's data center will be powered by a dedicated 932-megawatt natural gas-fired plant (the Greenlight Electricity Center) being developed by a consortium that includes Calgary-based Pembina Pipeline, Morgan Stanley Infrastructure Partners, and Kineticor Asset Management. Additionally, the facility will utilize a closed-loop cooling system that does not draw water from surrounding municipal sources, addressing growing environmental concerns over AI's resource consumption.

#### [Strategic Impact]

- **Technology & Ecosystem Perspective:** Highlights the industry-wide shift toward "power-first" data center strategies. Hyperscalers can no longer rely on public grids and must actively co-develop independent power generation to scale their next-generation AI clusters.
- **Business & Market Dynamics:** Meta's massive capital expenditure reinforces its commitment to open-source AI (Llama series) and independent infrastructure, reducing its long-term reliance on third-party cloud providers and strengthening its economic moat.
- **Infrastructure & Hardware Implications:** Validates the massive scale of next-generation AI clusters, requiring nearly a gigawatt of power for a single site. This drives immense demand for specialized industrial power equipment, pipeline infrastructure, and closed-loop cooling technologies.
- **Investment Strategy:** Highly bullish for energy infrastructure, pipeline operators, and independent power producers partnering with tech giants. Investors should target companies capable of delivering grid-independent, high-capacity power solutions directly to data center sites.
