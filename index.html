Together AI Raises $800 Million Series C at $8.3 Billion Valuation Led by Aramco Ventures

🔭 Overview

Together AI, the open-source AI cloud infrastructure startup, has officially closed an 800 million USD Series C funding round, bringing its post-money valuation to 8.3 billion USD.

The round was led by Aramco Ventures, the venture arm of Saudi energy giant Aramco, with participation from Nvidia, Vista Equity Partners, General Catalyst, Emergence Capital, March Capital, Pegatron, and SE Ventures. Together AI operates a specialized, high-performance cloud platform that enables enterprises to train and deploy open-source models—such as DeepSeek, Nemotron, and MiniMax—at scale. This Series C more than doubles Together AI's previous valuation of 3.3 billion USD, established during its 305 million USD Series B round in February 2025. The company asserts that its platform can significantly reduce inference costs relative to closed-model APIs—a value proposition that has driven annualized bookings past 1.15 billion USD. In addition to the equity raise, Together AI has secured commitments for more than 500 megawatts of compute capacity, capitalized independently by investors to support a projected 50-fold infrastructure expansion over the next five years.

Microsoft Launches "Microsoft Frontier Company" with $2.5 Billion Investment

🔭 Overview

Microsoft has officially launched "Microsoft Frontier Company," a new operating business backed by a 2.5 billion USD investment.

The unit will embed approximately 6,000 industry, engineering, and AI professionals directly within customer organizations to co-design, deploy, and continuously refine AI systems. Rodrigo Kede Lima, who previously led Microsoft's sales operations across the Americas and Asia, has been appointed President of the new operating unit. Microsoft Commercial Business CEO Judson Althoff emphasized that this initiative extends beyond what has become known as "Forward Deployed Engineering" (FDE), describing it as the largest, most capable, outcome-driven engineering organization in the industry. This announcement follows Amazon Web Services (AWS)'s own 1 billion USD investment in a forward-deployed engineering unit two days earlier, reflecting a broader industry recognition that the primary bottleneck to enterprise AI adoption is no longer model access, but the complex integration of AI into legacy corporate workflows.

This marks a major evolution in Nvidia's business model, transitioning from a pure hardware vendor to an active financial partner with a direct stake in its customers' operational utilization.
