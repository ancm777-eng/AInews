Illinois Governor Signs Landmark AI Safety Act Mandating Independent Audits of Frontier Models

🔭 Overview

Illinois Governor JB Pritzker has officially signed the Artificial Intelligence Safety Measures Act into law, establishing a highly aggressive regulatory precedent for the governance of advanced AI systems.

Modeled after similar legislative efforts in California and New York, the law positions Illinois as the first state in the nation to mandate independent, conflict-free third-party audits of frontier AI models for developers with annual revenues exceeding 500 million USD. Under the new statute, which is scheduled to take effect on January 1, 2027, developers operating within the state must publicly disclose their safety and security practices, report significant AI safety incidents, and maintain rigorous internal compliance programs. Bipartisan support for the bill was exceptionally strong: it passed unanimously in the House, and only five Republican senators voted against it in the Senate. Notably, frontier AI labs OpenAI and Anthropic both supported the bill during its legislative path, signaling a willingness to accept coordinated state-level frameworks over a fragmented regulatory patchwork. Lawmakers estimate that Illinois, California, and New York combined account for roughly 40% of the United States AI market, effectively establishing a de facto national standard in the absence of comprehensive federal regulations.
