Portland General Electric Files for 29% Rate Hike on Data Centers under Oregon's POWER Act

Source: Portland General Electric (PGE) / OPB / KGW Date: 2026-06-04 Category: Infrastructure
🔭 Overview

Portland General Electric (PGE), Oregon's largest electric utility, has formally filed for regulatory approval of a dramatic 29% electricity-rate increase specifically targeted at large-load data center customers.

Filed under Docket UM2377, the rate changes are designed to implement Oregon's landmark POWER Act, which requires large industrial energy users consuming more than 20 megawatts of power to pay for their share of electricity usage and grid infrastructure upgrades. To protect residential and small business ratepayers from subsidizing the massive grid expansion driven by high-density AI facilities, the proposal simultaneously introduces rate decreases for other classes, including a 1.3% reduction for residential customers and a 3.7% reduction for small businesses. PGE's data center electricity consumption has skyrocketed from 50 average megawatts in early 2020 to over 300 average megawatts in mid-2025, a level of demand comparable to roughly 240,000 households. This filing represents a profound shift in utility economics, establishing a "growth pays for growth" framework that holds tech giants financially responsible for the physical costs of the AI boom.
