3. IBM and U.S. Department of Commerce Partner to Establish America's First Pure-Play Quantum Foundry (Anderon) via Proposed $1 Billion CHIPS Award

🔭 Overview

IBM and the U.S. Department of Commerce have signed a Letter of Intent to establish Anderon, America's first purpose-built, pure-play quantum chip foundry.

Headquartered in Albany, New York, the standalone company will operate an advanced 300mm wafer foundry dedicated entirely to producing quantum wafers, initially focusing on superconducting qubits and supporting electronics. The venture is backed by a proposed 1 billion USD investment in CHIPS and Science Act incentives from the Department of Commerce, matched by 1 billion USD in cash from IBM, alongside key intellectual property, assets, and specialized talent. This landmark proposal is the largest piece of a broader 2 billion USD Department of Commerce initiative under which nine quantum companies, including GlobalFoundries receiving 375 million USD, and others like D-Wave, Rigetti, Infleqtion, Atom Computing, PsiQuantum, and Quantinuum receiving approximately 100 million USD each, are slated for federal support. Anderon will offer wafer fabrication services to multiple quantum technology vendors globally, establishing a domestic manufacturing base to secure U.S. leadership in advanced computing, which is projected to generate up to 850 billion USD in economic value by 2040.
