Meta Disables "Muse Image" Feature on Instagram Following Severe Backlash Over Privacy and Remixing

Meta Platforms has officially disabled a core feature of its newly launched "Muse Image" model on Instagram, less than a week after its high-profile rollout.

The decision, announced late on Friday, July 10, 2026, follows intense public backlash and pressure from advocacy groups such as SAG-AFTRA. Muse Image, the first image-generation model integrated directly into Meta AI, allowed users to reference and remix public Instagram photos of other users via @-mentions to generate customized derivative images. While Meta framed the feature as a creative tool, users quickly discovered that their personal, publicly posted photos were being used as direct reference material for AI-generated deepfakes and derivative works without explicit consent. In a statement, Meta acknowledged that the feature "missed the mark" on user privacy and confirmed it has been disabled globally, underscoring the growing friction between social media giants seeking to monetize user data and rising demand for digital privacy.
