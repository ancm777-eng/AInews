Gimlet Labs has officially emerged from stealth to announce an $80 million Series A funding round, led by Menlo Ventures, to rapidly scale the industry's first true multi-silicon inference cloud.

The fundamental industry pain point driving this innovation is the extreme economic and thermal inefficiency of utilizing homogeneous hardware — specifically expensive, power-hungry advanced GPUs like the H100 or Blackwell series — for complex, multi-step agentic workflows that encompass compute-bound prompt prefill, memory-bound sequential decode, and network-bound tool calls. Gimlet Labs introduces a decisive strategic shift through a proprietary software stack that automatically slices and orchestrates generative models across diverse hardware architectures in real-time, pairing high-end GPUs for prefill with legacy silicon, CPUs, and specialized SRAM-centric accelerators such as Cerebras and d-Matrix for rapid decoding. The startup reportedly achieved eight-figure revenues within just five months of operation and secured binding contracts with top-tier frontier AI labs and hyperscalers, as inference volumes surge to quadrillions of tokens per month.

Gimlet Labs' multi-silicon orchestration platform introduces significant deflationary pressure on inference costs, directly challenging the assumption that every AI workload requires the absolute latest generation of monolithic general-purpose GPUs. By proving that large language models can be dynamically sliced so that optimal chips handle specific phases of generation, Gimlet fundamentally extends the useful lifecycle of older silicon generations, drastically improving return on invested capital for existing enterprise data center deployments. The ability to route memory-bound decoding tasks to ultra-fast, statically scheduled SRAM accelerators further validates the multi-billion dollar valuations of custom silicon startups competing against NVIDIA, and accelerates the commercial viability of autonomous AI agents currently constrained by the latency profiles of traditional cloud infrastructure.
