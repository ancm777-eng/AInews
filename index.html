Coming in at a colossal 975 billion total parameters, Inkling is a natively multimodal Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) transformer that utilizes only 41 billion active parameters per token, balancing computational efficiency with high-end reasoning capabilities. Crucially, Thinking Machines has designed Inkling to be highly customizable via its "Tinker" platform, allowing enterprises to fine-tune the model on their own proprietary workflows rather than relying on frozen, centralized APIs. The release represents a major boost for the Western open-weights ecosystem, which has struggled to keep pace with Chinese open-source models following Meta's shift toward a proprietary approach. Early developer reception has been highly enthusiastic, with day-zero integration already live across major infrastructure platforms including vLLM, SGLang, Together AI, and Unsloth.

Nokia Launches Industry's First Commercial AI-RAN Platform with Nvidia Aerial

🔭 Overview

Nokia has officially announced the launch of the telecommunications industry's first commercial AI-RAN (Radio Access Network) platform, marking one of the most significant architectural shifts in mobile network design in decades.

Developed in partnership with Nvidia, the platform integrates Nokia's AI-native anyRAN software with Nvidia's Aerial AI-RAN platform, moving telecom providers away from custom, single-purpose silicon toward GPU-powered merchant silicon. By running compute-intensive AI algorithms at radio timescales, the platform aims to deliver more than 100% spectral efficiency gains by 2028, effectively doubling the data capacity operators can extract from their existing spectrum assets. Nokia is offering three distinct deployment paths to accommodate different operator requirements, including a GPU-powered AirScale capacity plug-in for existing deployments, a standalone AI-RAN node, and GPU-powered commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) server options for cloud-native operators. The platform will be monetized through a software subscription model, with pilot deployments scheduled for late 2026 and a general commercial release in 2027. T-Mobile US has been confirmed as the first operator to initiate field trials, signaling strong commercial interest in software-defined, AI-native network upgrades. This launch is a critical milestone for Nokia's CEO Justin Hotard, who folded the mobile business into a new Mobile Infrastructure segment. The Nvidia partnership sits at the center of this repair job, backed by a $1 billion investment from Nvidia for a 3% stake in October 2025.

3M and Microsoft Partner to Deploy Expanded Beam Optical (EBO) Technology in Azure Data Centers

🔭 Overview

Industrial conglomerate 3M and Microsoft have announced a major strategic partnership focused on advancing AI data center infrastructure and accelerating enterprise transformation.

Under the agreement, Microsoft's Azure will become the first hyperscale cloud provider to deploy 3M's proprietary Expanded Beam Optical (EBO) technology in its data centers. Traditional fiber optic connectors rely on direct-contact connections, which are highly sensitive to dust and contamination, requiring frequent cleaning and inspection in high-density environments. In contrast, 3M's EBO technology uses an expanded beam optical interface instead of direct contact, making fiber connections faster to install, more tolerant of contamination, and easier to maintain. Microsoft reported that early testing of 3M's EBO technology demonstrated the potential to significantly reduce network deployment timelines and improve signal reliability under demanding data center conditions. In return, 3M will implement Microsoft's AI and digital platforms across its global business functions, deploying AI agents to automate customer order management, credit checks, and financial auditing.
