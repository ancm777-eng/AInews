1. NVIDIA Launches Vera Rubin Supercomputing Platform Delivering 7 Exaflops of AI for Science

🔭 Overview

At the ISC High Performance 2026 conference in Hamburg, Germany, NVIDIA officially introduced the NVIDIA Vera Rubin platform, a revolutionary rack-scale supercomputing architecture designed specifically for converged high-performance computing (HPC) and agentic AI workloads.

The platform combines NVIDIA Rubin GPUs and NVIDIA Vera CPUs connected via high-speed NVLink-C2C interconnects, ConnectX-9 SuperNICs, and BlueField-4 data processing units (DPUs) in a direct liquid-cooled architecture. Built to address the physical limits of traditional simulation and scientific computing, Vera Rubin delivers an unprecedented 7 exaflops of AI performance for science (using the low-precision NVFP4 format) and 5 petaflops of native double-precision (FP64) computing in a single rack-scale configuration. Major global system manufacturers, including Dell Technologies, Supermicro, HPE, GIGABYTE, and Bull, immediately announced custom high-density systems based on the Vera Rubin NVL4 platform, scaling up to 144 GPUs per rack. Prominent research institutions, such as the Leibniz Supercomputing Centre (LRZ), the National Energy Research Scientific Computing Center (NERSC) at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, and Los Alamos National Laboratory, have already committed to deploying these next-generation supercomputers to advance climate modeling, quantum chemistry, and national security workloads.
