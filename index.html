New York Imposes Nation's First Statewide Moratorium on Hyperscale Data Centers

🔭 Overview

New York Governor Kathy Hochul has signed Executive Order No. 62, enacting the nation's first statewide temporary moratorium on the construction of new hyperscale data centers.

The order suspends discretionary permitting by the New York State Department of Environmental Conservation (DEC) for any new data center designed to consume 50 megawatts (MW) or more of power. The one-year pause is intended to provide state regulators, led by the Department of Public Service (DPS), with the time required to conduct a Generic Environmental Impact Statement (GEIS) and establish stringent standards governing the environmental, grid, and water impacts of these facilities. The decision arrives amid a substantial surge in data center load requests, with nearly 12 gigawatts (GW) of capacity currently sitting in the New York Independent System Operator (NYISO) interconnection queue—more than 8 GW of which entered in 2025 alone, according to NYISO's official queue data. Technology companies have cautioned that the moratorium will stifle local job growth and cede leadership in the AI race; however, public concern over rising consumer utility bills and significant water depletion from cooling requirements has compelled the state to act. Notably, the state legislature has separately proposed legislation defining hyperscale at a lower threshold of 20 MW, underscoring mounting political pressure to regulate AI infrastructure.
