The plaintiffs allege that the ChatGPT maker has engaged in "discovery misconduct" and "chosen obstruction" regarding the release of critical datasets and ChatGPT logs that could demonstrate how OpenAI and its business partner, Microsoft, built their AI models using millions of copyrighted news articles. The motion accuses OpenAI of concealing and destroying evidence demonstrating how ChatGPT was trained on "stolen journalism," following a recent deposition of an OpenAI employee that allegedly contradicted the company's earlier claims. The plaintiffs are seeking sanctions including attorneys' fees, as well as a finding that the company had the technical ability to search its training data and intentionally withheld or destroyed evidence.
