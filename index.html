US Federal Energy Regulatory Commission Orders Grid Operators to Speed Power to AI Data Centers

🔭 Overview

The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC) has unanimously voted to issue tailored "show-cause" orders to the six largest U.S. regional grid operators, including PJM, MISO, SPP, CAISO, ISO-NE, and NYISO.

The historic orders compel grid operators to justify within 60 days why their existing utility pricing structures can accommodate massive data centers or immediately reform them to accelerate grid integration. Additionally, grid operators must submit a mandatory reliability report within 30 days detailing how they will secure sufficient generation capacity to support large loads. This aggressive regulatory intervention, taken under Section 206 of the Federal Power Act, directly fulfills a request by Energy Secretary Chris Wright to help the United States better compete with China for AI superiority by eliminating the speculative interconnection queue backlog that has severely bottlenecked the AI data center buildout. Rather than offering a simple fast-track, the ruling focuses on establishing clear cost allocation and preventing cost-shifting to retail consumers, thereby forcing grid operators to build a more transparent, efficient, and reliable framework for connecting gigawatt-scale AI infrastructure.
