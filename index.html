Microsoft Quietly Shifts Thousands of Office Prompts to In-House MAI Models to Slash AI Costs

🔭 Overview

Microsoft has quietly begun routing tens of thousands of AI prompts per week within its core Microsoft 365 applications, specifically Excel and Outlook, to its internally developed Microsoft AI (MAI) models.

This strategic shift, first reported by Bloomberg, represents a significant departure from the company's previous heavy reliance on external frontier models from OpenAI and Anthropic to power its Copilot features. Led by Microsoft AI CEO Mustafa Suleyman, the initiative is driven by the escalating cost of AI tokens at corporate scale, which has squeezed margins and prompted the company to seek self-reliance. While the current volume of MAI-routed prompts represents a relatively small fraction of Microsoft's overall AI workload, the transition is actively expanding. The company has already integrated specialized in-house models, such as MAI-Image-2.5 in PowerPoint and the recently released MAI-Transcribe-1.5 in Teams, to handle finite, well-defined tasks. This shift marks the first visible production off-ramp for Microsoft's high-profile partnership with OpenAI, demonstrating how mature enterprise software providers are optimizing inference costs by routing routine tasks away from expensive external frontier providers.
