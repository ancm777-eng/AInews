BREAKTHROUGH
📅 March 24–27, 2026 Source: Google Research / Taipei Times ⬤ HIGH Confidence 7-Day Exception

The persistent memory bottleneck in AI inference has long been a primary limiter of context scale and hardware utilization.

On March 24, 2026, Google Research announced TurboQuant, a suite of compression techniques designed to address the "memory tax" of Large Language Model (LLM) inference. The suite, which includes the PolarQuant and Quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss (QJL) algorithms, enables a sixfold reduction in the key-value (KV) cache memory footprint while maintaining zero accuracy loss. This breakthrough allows high-dimensional vectors to be stored with minimal bits by reallocating information density — representing ordinary regions with low bits while retaining precision for outliers.

The announcement triggered an immediate and significant selloff in the global memory chip sector on March 26–27, with Samsung Electronics falling 5% and SK Hynix dropping over 6% in Seoul as investors weighed the potential for reduced memory demand in future data center builds.
