AMD Launches Instinct MI350P PCIe AI Accelerator for Enterprise Servers

Source: Network World Date: 2026-05-07 Status: Official Product Launch
🔭 Overview

Addressing the critical need for flexible, on-premises artificial intelligence infrastructure, AMD has officially launched the Instinct MI350P, its first PCIe-based Instinct accelerator in nearly four years.

While hyperscalers rely on massive, liquid-cooled server racks with integrated OAM modules, traditional enterprise data centers often lack the power and cooling capacity to support such dense configurations. The MI350P solves this bottleneck by offering a full-height, full-length dual-slot drop-in card that fits into standard air-cooled servers, allowing companies to gradually scale their AI capabilities without overhauling their existing physical infrastructure. Equipped with 144 gigabytes of HBM3E memory running at up to 4 terabytes per second, the card delivers an estimated 2,299 TFLOPS and up to 4,600 peak TFLOPS at MXFP4 precision. Crucially, a single GPU is capable of handling inference for large language models ranging from 200 to 250 billion parameters. This launch democratizes access to high-performance AI compute, enabling mid-market enterprises to run sophisticated agentic workflows entirely on-premises while directly competing with NVIDIA's RTX Pro 6000 Server PCIe offerings.
