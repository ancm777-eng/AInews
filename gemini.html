1. Release of Google Gemma 4: The On-Device Agentic Standard

1. Overview

Google's release of the Gemma 4 open model family on April 2, 2026, marks the definitive arrival of high-performance agentic AI on edge devices, fundamentally shifting the demand away from cloud-only inference. Gemma 4 is built on the same research foundation as Gemini 3 and introduces a hybrid attention mechanism that interleaves local sliding-window attention with full global attention. This architectural refinement, paired with Proportional RoPE (p-RoPE), enables a context window of 256K tokens for workstation models and 128K for edge variants. Google has optimized Gemma 4 for the Armv9 architecture's Scalable Matrix Extension 2 (SME2), delivering significant prefill performance gains on the latest mobile NPUs according to Arm's official newsroom. The model family includes a standout 26B Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) variant that activates only 3.8 billion parameters per token, achieving performance competitive with dense models many times its size while significantly reducing the "token tax" associated with continuous background agents.
