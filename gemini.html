Key specifications of the SN50 include:

[Overview]

Mamba-3, published as a conference paper at ICLR 2026 by researchers from CMU and Princeton — with support from Together AI and Cartesia AI — represents the most significant challenge to Transformer dominance in 2026.

[Strategic Impact]

It addresses the memory-bound nature of inference by replacing the attention mechanism's quadratic computational complexity and linearly growing KV cache of Transformers with a fixed-size latent state, enabling constant memory requirements across sequence length.

[Technical Deep Dive]

Key methodological improvements include:

  1. Exponential-Trapezoidal Discretization: A data-dependent convex combination method that eliminates the need for the short causal convolutions previously required by recurrent models. Mathematically equivalent to applying a data-dependent width-2 convolution within the core recurrence, this scheme makes the architecture more expressive without added inference cost.
  2. Complex-Valued State Tracking: Uses complex-valued SSMs — shown to be equivalent to real-valued SSMs with data-dependent Rotary Positional Embeddings — to improve arithmetic reasoning and parity problem solving, which were fundamental weaknesses in prior real-valued linear models.
  3. Multi-Input, Multi-Output (MIMO): Transforms the state update from an outer product to a matrix-matrix multiplication, increasing decoding FLOPs by up to 4x relative to Mamba-2 at a fixed state size. This exploits idle GPU tensor cores during decoding without increasing wall-clock latency.
